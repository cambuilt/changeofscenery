Intro to Charleston
  • Settled in 1670 across the Ashley River. First settlers were 150 Englishman, indentured servants and slaves.
  • Moved to the peninsula in 1680 since it offered a better harbor with two rivers that made it easier to defend and provided "highways" inland.
  • The only English walled city in the New World, encompassing a location most wanted by the French, Spanish, and Native Americans.
  • Historically the most battered city in America, under seige during two wars, threatened by WW II German U-Boats, slammed by widespread fires, earthquakes, floods, gas stations and Sears Roebuck.
  • Historically the fun city in America, with the first theatre where the first show was a comedy, first golf club, first musical society, 161 bars, and named after British King Charles II, the "Merry Monarch," a strong advocate of liveliness and hedonism.
